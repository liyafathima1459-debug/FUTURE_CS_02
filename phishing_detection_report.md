## 2. What is Phishing?
Phishing is a cyber attack where criminals send fake emails pretending to be
trusted organizations (banks, companies, government) to steal sensitive
information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

---

## 3. Phishing Email Samples & Analysis

### Sample 1 — Fake Bank Alert
- **Subject:** "URGENT: Your account has been suspended!"
- **Sender:** security@bank-alert-verify.com
- **Classification:** 🔴 Phishing
- **Indicators:**
  - Fake domain (bank-alert-verify.com)
  - Creates urgency and fear
  - Asks to click suspicious link
  - Poor grammar and spelling
- **What to do:** Delete immediately, do not click any links.

### Sample 2 — Fake IT Department
- **Subject:** "Your password will expire in 24 hours"
- **Sender:** it-support@company-helpdesk.net
- **Classification:** 🟡 Suspicious
- **Indicators:**
  - Unofficial domain
  - Requests password reset via link
  - Generic greeting ("Dear User")
- **What to do:** Verify with your IT department before clicking.

### Sample 3 — Legitimate Email
- **Subject:** "Your monthly invoice is ready"
- **Sender:** billing@amazon.com
- **Classification:** 🟢 Safe
- **Indicators:**
  - Official domain (amazon.com)
  - Personalized greeting
  - No urgent threats
  - No request for sensitive info
- **What to do:** Safe to open and read.

### Sample 4 — Fake Prize Winner
- **Subject:** "Congratulations! You won $1,000,000!"
- **Sender:** winner@lucky-prize-claim.com
- **Classification:** 🔴 Phishing
- **Indicators:**
  - Too good to be true
  - Unknown sender domain
  - Asks for personal/bank details
  - No prior contest entry
- **What to do:** Delete immediately, never share personal details.

## 5. How to Identify Phishing Emails
1. **Check the sender's email domain** — fake domains look similar but different
2. **Look for urgency** — "Act now!" or "Your account will be deleted!"
3. **Hover over links** — check if URL matches the claimed website
4. **Check for grammar errors** — legitimate companies proofread emails
5. **Never share passwords** — no legitimate company asks for your password

---

## 6. Prevention Guidelines
- Enable **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** on all accounts
- Use a **spam filter** to block suspicious emails
- **Never click** links in unexpected emails
- **Report** suspicious emails to your IT/security team
- Keep your **antivirus software** updated
